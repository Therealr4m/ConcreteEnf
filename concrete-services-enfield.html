Common Problems Caused by Poor Shed Bases

When we turn up to jobs in Enfield, most of the issues with sheds aren’t actually the shed itself — it’s what’s underneath it. A lot of bases we replace or correct were never properly set out in the first place.

We regularly see sheds that have started to move, twist or drop at one corner because the base wasn’t fully compacted or levelled before installation.

What usually goes wrong

  • Doors catching or not lining up properly after a short period of time
  • Floors feeling uneven when you walk inside the shed
  • Water sitting around or underneath the structure after rain
  • Cracks forming where the ground wasn’t properly prepared
  • One side of the shed slowly dropping over time

When we install a concrete shed base, the whole focus is getting it flat, compacted and stable from the start so the shed sits properly and stays that way long-term.

Local ground considerations

Concrete Base Installation Enfield – Ground Conditions

Having a shed base installed in Enfield isn’t simply a case of measuring up and pouring concrete. Ground conditions across the borough can vary noticeably between streets, and that variation directly affects how a base should be installed for long-term stability.

In many parts of Enfield, clay-heavy soil is common. Clay reacts to changes in moisture, expanding during wetter periods and shrinking in dry spells. Over time, that natural movement can place stress on poorly installed slabs. When the base is installed properly it accounts for this from the outset, ensuring the finished structure remains stable season after season.

Drainage patterns also differ from garden to garden. Slight slopes, neighbouring properties, fencing lines, and existing patios can all influence how water moves through a space. A well-considered base installation positions the slab correctly within that environment, helping prevent standing water and reducing long-term moisture exposure around your building.

This is particularly important for heavier structures such as log cabins or insulated garden rooms, where consistent support is essential.

Every concrete base installation is therefore approached with local awareness and structural intent. We dont just want a neat finish on the day of installation, but a base thats going to perform reliably for years without shifting, cracking, or causing any issues for the structure above it.

How we install concrete shed bases in Enfield

What Happens On Site During a Shed Base Installation

When we arrive on site in Enfield, the first thing we do is assess the garden properly before anything is dug or poured. Every garden layout is different, so we plan the base around access, levels, and where the shed is actually going to sit.

Once everything is confirmed, we start by setting out the exact footprint of the shed base. This ensures the dimensions are correct and the finished slab matches the structure with no overhangs or misalignment.

Step-by-step on-site process

  • Marking out the exact shed base footprint on site
  • Excavating the area to the correct depth for stability
  • Installing and compacting a solid sub-base where required
  • Checking levels across the full area before concrete is poured
  • Pouring and finishing the concrete to a smooth, even surface

Every stage is done to make sure the final base is level, stable and ready to take the full weight of the shed without movement or settling over time.

Once finished, the area is left clean and the base is checked again for consistency so it’s ready for shed installation once cured.
