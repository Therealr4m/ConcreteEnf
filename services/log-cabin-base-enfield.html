Concrete bases for log cabins across Enfield

We fit strong long term concrete bases for log cabins, we come to your Enfield property fully equipped to fit your concrete base for a log cabin properly. From ground preparation through to the final finish, everything is planned around weight, load and long-term performance.

Log cabins are not lightweight garden structures. They require a base that holds firm, stays level and supports the full footprint evenly over time. That’s where proper preparation and correct installation makes all the difference.

Log cabin base requirements

Heavy-duty concrete bases built for log cabins

A log cabin base needs to handle far more weight than a standard shed. The full structure sits across the slab, meaning the load must be distributed evenly to avoid movement, sinking or long-term stress points.

Thickness & reinforcement options

  • Typically thicker slabs than standard shed bases
  • Steel mesh reinforcement for load distribution
  • Rebar options for heavier or larger cabins
  • Edge support to prevent cracking over time

The right setup depends on the size of your log cabin and how it will be used. A small garden cabin is very different to a fully insulated garden room.

Ground preparation

What sits under your base matters just as much

Most problems with log cabin bases don’t come from the concrete itself — they come from what’s underneath it. If the ground hasn’t been prepared properly, the slab will eventually move.

  • Excavation to correct depth
  • Weed membrane where required
  • Compacted sub-base for stability
  • Level checks before any concrete is poured

This is what gives the base its long-term strength, not just the concrete on top.

Drainage & stability

Keeping your log cabin dry and stable

Water is one of the main causes of long-term issues. A properly installed base includes fall planning so water drains away rather than sitting against the slab or timber structure.

Over time, poor drainage leads to moisture exposure, timber wear and ground movement. Getting this right at install stage prevents those issues completely.

Access & installation

Planning the install around your Enfield property

Most Enfield gardens have limited access. Side paths, tight spaces and neighbouring properties all affect how the job is planned and delivered.

Concrete delivery, timing and setup are coordinated so the base can be installed properly without delays or rushed work.

Common questions

Log cabin base FAQs

How thick should a log cabin base be?

It depends on the size and weight of the cabin, but log cabins typically require a thicker slab than standard sheds to handle the load properly.

Do I need reinforcement?

In most cases yes. Reinforcement helps spread the load and prevents cracking, especially for larger cabins.

What needs to be done before installation?

The area needs to be cleared and accessible. Ground preparation, excavation and sub-base installation are all handled as part of the job.
