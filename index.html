Shed base installation — step-by-step

Ground prep, load-bearing concrete and drainage

Every Enfield base is designed with four engineering decisions in mind:

1 — Ground preparation

Good concrete starts with the ground. The team starts off by removing topsoil, creating a compacted crushed stone sub-base then adding a geotextile membrane to reduce weed growth and movement. Proper sub-base compaction reduces settlement and keeps the base level for decades.

2 — Reinforcement & thickness

Choice of reinforcement depends on expected loads. For a typical garden shed used to store tools, mesh reinforcement and 100–120mm concrete is common. For heavier structures (log cabins, workshops) thicker slabs with steel mesh or rebar to distribute load are used.

3 — Drainage & fall

Surface fall is arranged to direct water away from buildings. Where required channels will be cut or drainage points will be fitted. This avoids standing water and protects timber structures above.

4 — Finish & tolerances

All work is finished to a smooth, level surface so your shed doors close easily and floors sit flat. Tolerances are kept tight — All measurements will be double checked before finishing your concrete base and leaving site.
