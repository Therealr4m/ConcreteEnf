Shed base installation — step-by-step

Ground prep, load-bearing concrete and drainage

We design every base with four engineering decisions in mind:

1 — Ground preparation

Good concrete starts with the ground. We remove topsoil, create a compacted crushed stone sub-base and add a geotextile membrane to reduce weed growth and movement. Proper sub-base compaction reduces settlement and keeps the base level for decades.

2 — Reinforcement & thickness

Choice of reinforcement depends on expected loads. For a typical garden shed used to store tools, mesh reinforcement and 100–120mm concrete is common. For heavier structures (log cabins, workshops) we use thicker slabs with steel mesh or rebar to distribute load.

3 — Drainage & fall

Surface fall is arranged to direct water away from buildings. Where required we cut channels or fit drainage points. This avoids standing water and protects timber structures above.

4 — Finish & tolerances

We finish to a smooth, level surface so your shed doors close easily and floors sit flat. Tolerances are kept tight — we measure and check before we leave.

Frequently asked — quick answers

How long does installation take?

Typical small shed bases can be completed in a day: prep in the morning and pour/finish in the afternoon. Larger or reinforced bases may need multiple days and longer curing.

Can I put a shed on paving or sleepers?

You can, but concrete bases give a totally level, long-lasting foundation. For timber sheds that store heavy loads, a concrete base is the lowest maintenance option.
